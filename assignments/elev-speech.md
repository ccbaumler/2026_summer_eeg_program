## Elevator Speeches:

{: .note}
> <a href="{{ '/resources/scientific-storytelling/' | relative_url }}">Further information on scientific storytelling may be found in resources on this site (here)</a>

In the sciences, it is important to develop several versions the covey the story of your research:

- **30-60 second version**: imagine you’re on an elevator with Bill Gates, who offers you $$$ if you can convince him that what you do is important
- **1–3-minute version**: you’re presenting your poster at a conference or in a bar with a colleague
- **10-minute version**: this is called a “conference talk.” It’s an expanded version of the shorter versions

{: .important-title}
> How to do this? Tell them a story!
>
> I’m conducting research on ____________ .
>
> We know that ____________ (describe in one sentence a fact that’s background to your research question).
>
> AND we also know that ____________ (add another fact about your research question).
>
> (optionally, add another “AND” statement)
>
> BUT we don’t know ____________ (describe a gap in the knowledge).
>
> THEREFORE, I am ____________ (briefly describe what you’re doing).
>
> If you’ve set it up appropriately, your audience should gasp in amazement. If you need to hit them over the head, add,
>
> THIS IS IMPORTANT BECAUSE ____________ .

## Weekly Stand-up **Only if there is time at the end**{: .label .label-red }

1. What went well this past week? What did I accomplish?
2. What was challenging this week? Where am I still stuck? How do you plan to address it?
3. What are my goals for next week?
4. Optional grab bag: What’s something fun I learned this week?
